Driver Cognitive Load Classification

HFAST Lab, University of Toronto
Toronto, Canada
July 2019 - October 2019

In semi-auto driving, it is necessary to estimate whether the driver is focusing. A variety of measures were used to estimate driversâ€™ cognitive workload, for example, heart rate (HR), galvanic skin response (GSR) and Electroencephalography (EEG). Previous methods fail to take time sequence into consideration and cause the limitation of the accuracy. Consequently, we proposed a new method based on Long Short-Term Memory. Experiments on the actual dataset show that the accuracy has been greatly improved, from 79.4% to 88.5%.

My job: Processing the collected data, building and optimizing the Machine Learning Model, analysing the result, and writing part of the paper.

SAR Signal Finely Focusing

Radar Institute, Shanghai Jiao Tong University
Shanghai, China
March 2018 - Janurary 2019

With the continuous development of relative technology, more and more SAR(Synthetic Aperture Radar) begin to be built on small platforms, making it difficult to carry sensors and therefore, to obtain the finely focusing SAR signal. Consequently, we used Very Deep Neural Network, which performs quite well on Image Super-Resolution, to deal with coarse focused SAR signals. Results show that the texture features of images are restored.

My job: Almost all of the work, including simulation of coarse focusing, making the dataset, using Amazon Web Servise to train, analysing the result, and writing the paper.
